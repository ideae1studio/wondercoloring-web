Secretarybird

Sagittarius serpentarius

Key Characteristics

  • The Secretarybird is a large bird of prey, easily recognized by its long legs and crest of black feathers.
  • It gets its name because the black feathers behind its head look like old-fashioned secretaries used to tuck pens behind their ears!
  • Unlike most birds of prey, the Secretarybird hunts mostly on the ground, walking long distances every day.

Peculiar Facts (Fun Facts!)

  • This bird is famous for stomping on snakes with its powerful legs and feet, delivering a quick, strong blow.
  • Their long legs help them see over tall grasses and keep them safe from small predators.
  • They build huge nests, sometimes 5 feet wide, high up in acacia trees.
